Client - Myself
Period - September 2018

Could Be Worse

An early project of mine, the goal was to create an online magazine, write the content for it, and make it responsive. The simplicity of the site was done to draw attention to the content, which in this case is the articles.

The bottom navigation was to remove unnecessary clutter along the page, and to make sure people read the articles before proceeding to the next one. Since there are only 3 articles written for the page, this concise navigation method means the site is easy to navigate.

Client - Tattoo Studio 96
Period - November 2018

Tattoo Studio 96

Modern and contemporary design is always cool, and it’s even cooler when the business already reflects these properties. By collaborating with Tattoo Studio 96, I was able to produce a clean and up-to-date site what matched their style.

The navigation on this site is a side menu which pops out from the left when accessed. This navigation was primarily chosen because of the mobile first design approach used for this project. However I later found that the navigation translates quite well to the desktop version of the site as well.

Given that it is a one-page layout, the navigation simply scrolls to the desired destination. When open, the menu button disappears, so that it can indicate to the user that to close it they can tap (or click) anywhere outside the menu.

Client - Dea Marie
Period - February 2019

Web Documentary

Creating a documentary for the artist Dea Marie was a highlight of my professional career. Getting to know her and learning enough to tell her story was an amazing experience.

The website is styled to be clean and minimalistic, with neutral colors that blend together with the images used, and smooth animations to make for a more seamless user experience.

The videos were directed and edited by me as well. It was once again a challenge to capture her artistic essence as well as her every day life, but it was a challenge I took to with pleasure.

About Me

Hi, as you might already know my name is Matija Hrnjak, and I am a 21-year-old Multimedia student living in Aarhus, Denmark. I am an aspiring UX Designer, hoping to find a future in the field.

I tend to be very curious, and this curiosity leads me to being a quick learner and a good researcher. I’m also very passionate about film, typography, fashion, and hip-hop music. However as someone who is constantly hungry for knowledge, I know that there is always more to learn and ways to improve, which leads to my life philosophy of constant improvement no matter what I do.

My productivity is a product of my environment, in group projects I tend to take a role as a leader, however I have no issues with following, and in solo efforts I tend to zone out everything and work as hard as I can. Basically, I'm like water: No matter what glass you pour me in I’ll fit.
